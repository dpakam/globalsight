Analog B tab (W2487)

Use this tab to define analog data parameters for the Waters 2487 Dual l Absorbance Detector.

Output mode

Specifies the analog output parameter to monitor. The selected parameter is output to the channel A and/or channel B 2-V and 10-mV terminals on the rear of the detector (for connection to other devices such as a chart recorder). See the Waters 2487 Dual l Absorbance Detector Operator’s Guide for more parameter details. Parameter selections vary according to the Wavelength Mode (Single or Dual) selected in the General tab. Choices:

  • Absorbance A (Ch1) (Single and Dual l Modes) – Represents the current absorbance at wavelength 1.
  • Absorbance B (Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the current absorbance at wavelength 2.
  • Log Absorbance A (Ch1) (Single and Dual l Modes) – Represents the log (base 10) of absorbance at wavelength 1, allowing small and large peaks to be viewed on one chromatogram.
  • Log Absorbance B (Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the log (base 10) of absorbance at wavelength 2, allowing small and large peaks to be viewed on one chromatogram.
  • Sample Energy A (Ch1) (Single and Dual l Modes) – Represents the energy from the sample side of the flow cell (at wavelength 1).
  • Sample Energy B (Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the energy from the sample side of the flow cell (at wavelength 2).
  • Reference Energy A (Ch1) (Single and Dual l Modes) – Represents the energy from the reference side of the flow cell (at wavelength 1).
  • Reference Energy B (Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the energy from the reference side of the flow cell (at wavelength 2).
  • Maxplot A, B (Ch1, Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the maximum absorbance value for each sample component. The higher of the two absorbance values (for each component) is output on the selected channel.
  • Ratioplot A/B (Ch1/Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the ratio of absorbances at the two selected wavelengths.
  • Ratioplot B/A (Ch2/Ch1) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the ratio of absorbances at the two selected wavelengths.
  • Difference Plot A–B (Ch1–Ch2) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the A–B difference in absorbance at the two selected wavelengths.
  • Difference Plot B–A (Ch2–Ch1) (Dual l Mode only) – Represents the B–A difference in absorbance at the two selected wavelengths.

Note: Monitor a second parameter through the channel A and/or channel B 2 V and 10 mV terminals by selecting an Output Mode in the Analog A tab. Also, you can monitor up to two additional parameters (over the IEEE-488 interface) by selecting a Data Mode in the Channel 1 and Channel 2 tabs.

AUFS

Relationship between the absorbance and the output voltage. The output voltage reaches full scale (2 V or 10 mV) when the absorbance equals the AUFS setting.

Default: 2.0000 AUFS

Polarity

Polarity (+ or –) of the 2-V and 10-mV analog outputs to a chart recorder. Specifying + produces a normal chromatogram. Specifying – produces an inverted chromatogram.

Default: +

Absorbance offset (AU)

Optional offset in absorbance for adjusting the analog outputs. An absorbance offset of 1.0 offsets the charted absorbance by 1.0 AU.

Default: 0.000

Voltage offset (mV)

Optional voltage offset for the analog output signals. This offset adjusts the 2-V output by the entered value, and adjusts the 10-mV output by 1/200 of the entered value.

Default: 0

Chart mark enable

Enables a chart mark each time the 2487 Detector receives a chart mark request through the keypad or by an event input (contact closure).

Choices: Enabled or Disabled

Default: Enabled

Ratio values

Specify ratio-plotting parameters in this section of the property page. These parameters are used only when you have selected Ratioplot A/B or Ratioplot B/A as an Output Mode (see above).

Note: In addition to Scaling Minimum and Maximum parameters, set the Ratio Minimum parameter in the Channel 1 and Channel 2 tabs.

Scaling minimum

Lower limit of the ratio output (Ratioplot A/B or Ratioplot B/A). This setting is equal to 0.0 V on the output scale.

Default: 0.00

Scaling maximum

Upper (full-scale) limit of the ratio output (Ratioplot A/B or Ratioplot B/A).

Default: 2.00
