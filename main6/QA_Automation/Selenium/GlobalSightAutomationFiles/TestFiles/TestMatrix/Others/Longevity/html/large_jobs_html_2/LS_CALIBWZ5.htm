LS Calibration wizard - Calibration Constants page

This page displays the LS calibration constant and the concentration channel calibration constant calculated during the calibration process. It also indicates whether the concentration channel is connected to an RI detector.

The two constant fields are always editable and blank if the values are not successfully calculated. You can save the calibration with blank constants. However, if the LS calibration constant is blank, then you cannot calculate molecular weights from LS data because the LS detector was not calibrated.

Note: The calibration constants must be positive numbers. Zero is not a valid entry.

If the concentration channel is connected to a calibrated RI detector and the concentration channel constant field contains a value, you can calculate molecular weights from your LS data when you did not enter a concentration during sample loading or in the Alter Sample window. This approach is useful when you do not know the accurate concentration of your sample or you are having solubility problems with your sample.

Click Finish to close the wizard. If the RI Calibration Constant is calculated, a message box appears asking if you want to copy the RI Calibration Constant to the instrument method. Click Yes.
