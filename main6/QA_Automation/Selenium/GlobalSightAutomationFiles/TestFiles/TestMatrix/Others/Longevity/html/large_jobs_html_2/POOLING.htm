Bath A or B Pooling page

Use this page to enter sample pooling parameters for Bath A or B. This page appears only when you selected a Dissolution option and the 2690D/2695D installed check box in the Select Sample Set Method Type page.

In pooling, an aliquot is drawn from each vessel and transferred to its corresponding vials in the sample compartment. A small sample amount from each vial to be pooled is drawn into the sample loop and injected as one sample onto the column. Pooling saves time because samples are not analyzed separately.

Note: The total amount of pooled sample collected in the sample loop cannot exceed the size of the sample loop. For example, six samples of 10 mL totals 60 mL which is drawn into the sample loop. The standard sample loop size is 100 mL.

Pool injections

Enables the pooling of samples and thus the remaining fields are enabled.

Pooled vessels numbers

Specifies the vessel numbers or vessel ranges from which samples are drawn from the vial, and pooled before they are injected on the column. The allowable range is from 1 to either 6 or 8. Separate the vessel ranges with commas, for example, 1–6, or 1–8, or 1,3,5–8.

Sample vial amount

Specifies the volume (in microliters) of sample added to each vial.

Note: If you have a 2690D/2695D, the sample vial amount to be pooled must match the injection volume as specified on the next page of the Sample Set Method wizard (Sample Info page).

Entries: 1.0 to 9999.9

Typical entries: 0.00 to 1.5

Interval between pooling and injecting

Specifies the amount of delay time (in minutes) to wait before injecting the samples after pooling is complete. The interval or delay time allows the sample to diffuse in the sample loop.
