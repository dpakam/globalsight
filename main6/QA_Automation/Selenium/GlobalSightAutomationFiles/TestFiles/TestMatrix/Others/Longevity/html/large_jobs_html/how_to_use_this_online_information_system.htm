How to use this online information system

Note to writers: You must update the content in this topic to reflect how functionality in the viewer you are using to display Help content to users works. For example, the viewer used to display Help in Flare's standard viewer differs from the Waters proprietary viewer used for UNIFI Help. The information included in this topic is an example of common Help viewer functionality. It is not specific to the UNIFI Help viewer.

This topic database is part of the Online Information System. This system is designed to answer your questions while you work with products and the product suite. You can easily resize this information system window, move it, and hide or show the navigation pane as required.

Viewing information in this window

Use standard window-operating procedures to manipulate this window. For example, you can drag a corner of this window to resize it or click the Minimize button in the top-right corner to temporarily shrink it into a button at the bottom of your screen. Once you find the task-related information you need, click the Hide Navigation Pane icon in the top-left corner of the window to close the navigation panel on the left, keeping only the contents of the topic displayed. After using the information to help you complete your task, you can then click the Show Navigation Pane icon to redisplay the navigation pane if desired.

Navigating to a topic for complete details

The Table of Contents pane on the left side of the Help window contains a list of topics based on the common tasks you perform with this product suite.

  • Click the triangle symbol next to a section to show or hide the subsections and topics within it
  • Double-click a topic entry to display the related information in place of the text you are now reading

In the Search box above the Table of Contents pane, type a key word or phrase to locate a specific piece of information in this online information system.

Printing topic content

To print a single topic, click the Print toolbar button or right-click within the topic, and then click Print.

Tip: The header and footer information that appears at the top and bottom of the pages when you print a topic from the Help system is controlled by the same print settings you have defined for Internet Explorer. Refer to the Internet Explorer Help for information on how to specify what you want to print in the headers and footers of your topics.

Copying information contained in a topic

To copy information from one of the topics in this online information system into another file:

  • In the topic window, select the text you want to copy, right-click, and then select Copy from the shortcut menu that appears.
  • Open the file in which you want to copy the information, and position the cursor where desired.
  • From the Edit menu, click Paste.

Searching for information

When you want to quickly find information, you can scan the Table of Contents in this online information system or conduct a search for specific word(s).

To search for words in this online information system:

  • In the Search box above the Table of Contents pane, type the word(s) you want to find, and then click Search.

Tip: For more advanced searches, you can enter Boolean and wildcard expressions with your search criteria. Wildcard expressions allow you to search for one or more characters using a question mark or asterisk. For Boolean expressions, the AND, OR, NOT, and NEAR operators enable you to precisely define your search by creating a relationship between search terms. If no operator is specified, AND is used. For example, the query "acquisition method set" is equivalent to "acquisition AND method AND set." You can also use nested expressions to create complex searches for information. For example, "processing AND ((code OR field) NEAR peak)" finds topics containing the word "processing" along with the words "code" and "peak" close together, or containing "processing" along with the words "field" and "peak" close together.

Nested expressions:

Rule

Description

You can use parenthesis

The expressions in parentheses are evaluated before the rest of the query. For example, "regulatory practices and (audit trails)" will first look for topics with the words "audit trails"

If a query does not contain a nested expression, it is evaluated from left to right

For example, "editor NOT instrument OR method" finds topics containing the word "editor" without the word "instrument" or topics containing the word "method". On the other hand, "editor NOT (instrument OR method)" finds topics containing the word "editor" without either of the words "instrument" or "method".

You can use multiple levels of nesting

You cannot nest expressions more than five levels deep.
